---
title: "Post-installation"
description: "Essential steps after starting mailcow for the first time: change the admin password, configure DNS authentication records, and add your first domain and mailbox."
---

Complete these steps immediately after your first login to mailcow. Skipping them leaves your server insecure and unable to deliver mail reliably.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Change the admin password">
    The default admin credentials are `admin` / `moohoo`. These are public and must be changed before your mailcow instance handles any real mail.

    1. Log in to the mailcow UI at `https://your-hostname` with `admin` / `moohoo`.
    2. Click **Edit** next to the admin account in the top-right user menu, or navigate to **Access** > **Administrators**.
    3. Enter a new strong password and save.

    <Warning>
      Do not skip this step. The default password `moohoo` is documented publicly. Any server reachable from the internet with the default credentials is immediately at risk.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your TLS certificate">
    During startup, the ACME container (`acme-mailcow`) attempts to obtain a Let's Encrypt certificate for your mail hostname using HTTP-01 validation. This requires:

    - Port 80 reachable from the internet
    - Your hostname's A/AAAA record pointing to your server's public IP
    - `SKIP_LETS_ENCRYPT=n` in `mailcow.conf` (the default)

    To check whether the certificate was issued successfully, look at the ACME container logs:

    ```bash
    docker compose logs acme-mailcow
    ```

    A successful issuance will show the certificate being copied into place. Once issued, your browser should no longer show a security warning when visiting the UI.

    <Note>
      If you run a reverse proxy in front of mailcow, or if port 80 is occupied by another service, you may need to adjust your ACME configuration. See [SSL/TLS configuration](/configuration/ssl-tls) for alternative setups including DNS-01 challenge support.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your first domain">
    Before creating mailboxes, you must add the mail domain (for example, `example.com`) to mailcow.

    1. In the admin UI, navigate to **Configuration** > **Mail Setup**.
    2. Under the **Domains** tab, click **Add domain**.
    3. Enter your domain name and adjust quotas if needed, then click **Add domain and restart SOGo**.

    After adding the domain, you can create mailboxes for it:

    1. Click the **Mailboxes** tab.
    2. Click **Add mailbox**.
    3. Enter the local part (e.g., `alice`), select the domain, set a password, and save.

    For full details on domain and mailbox options, see [Domains and mailboxes](/administration/domains-mailboxes).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate DKIM keys and configure DNS records">
    Proper email authentication requires SPF, DKIM, and DMARC DNS records. Without them, your outbound mail is likely to be flagged as spam or rejected by receiving servers.

    **Generate a DKIM key in the mailcow UI:**

    1. Navigate to **Configuration** > **Mail Setup** > **Configuration** tab.
    2. Find the DKIM section, select your domain, choose a key length (2048 bits recommended), and click **Add**.
    3. Copy the generated DKIM public key value.

    **Add the following DNS records for each domain:**

    | Record type | Name | Value |
    |-------------|------|-------|
    | TXT | `example.com` | `v=spf1 mx ~all` — authorizes your mail server to send for this domain |
    | TXT | `dkim._domainkey.example.com` | The DKIM public key generated in the mailcow UI |
    | TXT | `_dmarc.example.com` | `v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@example.com` — start with `p=none` to monitor before enforcing |

    <Tip>
      Start with `p=none` in your DMARC policy so you can monitor reports without risking mail loss. Once you have confirmed your SPF and DKIM are working correctly, move to `p=quarantine` and then `p=reject`.
    </Tip>

    DNS changes may take up to 24–48 hours to propagate globally, though in practice they are often visible within minutes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test mail flow">
    After DNS propagates, verify that your server can send and receive mail correctly.

    **Send a test message:**

    Log in to the SOGo webmail at `https://your-hostname/SOGo` using a mailbox you created, and send a message to an external address (for example, a Gmail account). Check that it arrives and is not marked as spam.

    **Receive a test message:**

    Send a message from an external account to one of your mailboxes, then verify it appears in SOGo or your mail client.

    **Check authentication results:**

    Many services can help verify your SPF, DKIM, and DMARC setup. Send a message to a testing service and review the headers in the reply to confirm that SPF passes, DKIM is signed and verified, and DMARC aligns.

    <Note>
      If outbound mail is rejected or flagged as spam, check that your server's reverse DNS (PTR record) matches your mail hostname, and that your IP is not listed on common blocklists.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
